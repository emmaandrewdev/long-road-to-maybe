Quick reference

The IVF glossary

Every term, acronym, and abbreviation you'll encounter — explained plainly. Use the alphabet filter to find what you need, or download the full glossary from our Resources page.

AMH

Anti-Müllerian Hormone

A blood test used to estimate ovarian reserve — how many eggs you have remaining. A lower result doesn't mean you can't conceive, but it informs your clinic's approach to stimulation.

AFC

Antral Follicle Count

An ultrasound count of small follicles visible on your ovaries at the start of your cycle. Used alongside AMH to predict your response to stimulation drugs.

Blastocyst

Day 5–6 embryo

An embryo that has developed for five or six days and reached the blastocyst stage. These are generally considered to have a higher chance of successful implantation than earlier-stage embryos.

Embryo grading

Quality assessment

A system used by embryologists to assess embryo quality. Grades vary by clinic and stage, but broadly reflect cell number, symmetry, and fragmentation. A lower grade doesn't mean an embryo won't work.

FET

Frozen Embryo Transfer

A cycle in which a previously frozen embryo is thawed and transferred to the uterus. Outcomes for FET cycles are now comparable or superior to fresh transfers in many studies.

hCG

Human Chorionic Gonadotropin

The hormone detected by pregnancy tests, also used as a trigger shot to mature eggs before collection. Its presence is what causes the classic early pregnancy symptoms.

ICSI

Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection

A form of IVF in which a single sperm is injected directly into an egg, rather than sperm and egg being placed together. Most commonly recommended for male factor infertility.

PGT-A

Preimplantation Genetic Testing

Testing of embryos for chromosomal abnormalities before transfer. Evidence on its benefit for general IVF populations remains debated — it is more clearly indicated in specific circumstances.

Progesterone

Luteal support hormone

A hormone supplemented after egg collection and transfer to support the uterine lining. Progesterone causes symptoms (bloating, breast tenderness, fatigue) that closely mimic early pregnancy — making symptom-spotting unreliable during the TWW.

TWW

Two-Week Wait

The period between embryo transfer and the official pregnancy test date. Widely considered the most emotionally difficult part of an IVF cycle.

Chemical pregnancy

Early pregnancy loss

A very early pregnancy loss detected by a positive hCG test that does not progress to a clinical pregnancy. Often occurs before five weeks. The clinical term does not reflect the emotional weight of the experience for many people.

Trigger shot

Final maturation injection

An injection given approximately 36 hours before egg collection to trigger final maturation of follicles. Timing is precise — it is one of the most time-sensitive steps in an IVF cycle.
